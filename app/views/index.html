v4

{{ button({ "text": "Launch v4", "href": "v4/index" }) }}

Date started: 7/11/2019
Date ended: 6/12/2019

User testing (2)

  • Old Fire Station, Gipton - 26-27/11/2019, 8 users (60min sessions)
  • Old Fire Station, Gipton - 2-3/12/2019, 8 users (60min sessions)

Key Findings (5)

  • Partner service usability is just as important as DA journey
  • Users have different mental models around how to add a delegate - many would share login credentials
  • Once users found the area to add a delegate, they were more confident with adding another one
  • Users want to directly control who has access on their behalf
  • Users may need to be sign-posted to add a person in different parts of the partner service

Design Challenges (5)

  • Users need a clearer way to login to stepWell
  • Users need a way back to stepWell at the end of the process
  • Users want to understand delegates permissions earlier in the journey
  • Users may need to have the name displayed back to them earlier in the journey so that they know that they have entered in the right name
  • Make one single point of contact across the whole journey
v3

{{ button({ "text": "Launch v3", "href": "v3/index" }) }}

Date started: 1/10/2019 (ish)
Date ended: 7/11/2019

User testing (3)

  • Weetwood Hall - 7/10/2019, 7 users (40min sessions)
  • Old Fire Station, Gipton - 31/10/2019, 5 users (45min sessions)
  • Coercion group - early Nov, 2 users (15min sessions)

Key Findings (4)

  • Users were confused between who's health they were attempting to gain access of
  • Users didn't understand the difference between creating an NHS login and accepting the invite
  • Users didn't understand the difference between stepWell and NHS login
  • Coercion experts wasn't sure the current coercion message (in security email) was good enough

Design Challenges (3)

  • Redesign the email to help users better understand what they need to do
  • How can we explain to users the difference between NHS login, stepWell, accepting invite and creating NHS login.
  • Coercion needs to be looked at again - is the yellow box in the security email enough?

  • Change the service name from myHealth to something totally different (with no pronouns)
  • Improve the content and grouping of the relationship radios
